How to read the chart

The left scale is altitude in feet. Each horizontal reference plane shows where the airport sits, where the pressure altitude lies, and where the aircraft effectively performs based on the current temperature.

Pressure altitude

Pressure altitude is the altitude indicated when the altimeter is set to standard pressure. Lower QNH produces a higher pressure altitude; higher QNH produces a lower pressure altitude.

Density altitude

Density altitude increases when the air is warm, thin, or both. A higher density altitude generally means reduced aircraft performance during takeoff, climb, and landing.

This visualization is intended for planning and education. Always verify performance calculations with the approved aircraft documentation, airport data, and operational procedures.
